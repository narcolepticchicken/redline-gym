The contract does not address either of the two topics the procurement team specifically requires: (1) hosting location and the basis for cross-border transfers, and (2) certified deletion of Customer Data at exit.  

- **Hosting location / cross-border transfer basis**: Nowhere does the agreement identify where Customer Data will be stored, processed, or transferred across borders (e.g., specific countries, regions, or use of adequacy decisions, standard contractual clauses, etc.). This omission directly conflicts with the client’s stated need for visibility into data location and lawful transfer mechanisms for personal data.  
- **Certified deletion at exit**: The MSA contains no obligation for Vendor to delete Customer Data upon termination/expiration, nor any requirement to provide a certified record of deletion. Sections 11 (Customer Exit Right) and 12 (Transition Assistance) cover convenience termination and transition help but are silent on data destruction. The absence of a deletion-plus-certification clause means the client cannot demonstrate that personal data has been permanently removed at the end of the engagement, which the procurement brief explicitly mandates.  

Because the agreement is silent on these two material points, a careful reviewer must escalate both gaps.
