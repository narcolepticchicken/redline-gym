| 1 | §1 Confidential Information | *"that is marked confidential at the time of disclosure or confirmed as confidential in writing within ten days after disclosure"* | R-001 | high |

**Reasoning per item:**
1. Playbook requires coverage regardless of marking; contract gates the definition on marking or a 10-day written-confirmation window — narrows scope of what counts as Confidential Information at all.
2. Purpose is defined narrowly ("evaluating, negotiating, and, if agreed, performing the contemplated data integration"), but the use grant in §2 tacks on open-ended "commercial opportunities" language exceeding that defined Purpose.
3. §3 has the need-to-know qualifier the playbook wants, but drops the second, independently required element: Representatives must be contractually/professionally bound to protect at the same level.
4. §4 sets only a subjective "same as own" standard with no objective reasonable-care minimum — a recipient with lax internal practices would satisfy the clause while giving no real protection.
5. Playbook wants notice *before* disclosure (to allow Discloser to intervene) plus cooperation; §5 only promises notice *after* disclosure has already occurred, and no cooperation duty at all.
6. Playbook fallback is "promptly"; 90 days is a materially long return/destroy window.
