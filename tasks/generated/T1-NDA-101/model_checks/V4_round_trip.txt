The shipped contract deviates from the playbook in the following ways:

- Section 2, quote: “Recipient may use Confidential Information for the Purpose and for internal business planning, product evaluation, and commercial opportunity analysis related to the parties' discussions.”  
  **Violated rule**: R-004 (Use must be limited to evaluating/negotiating/performing the transaction; these added uses go beyond that scope).

- Section 4, quote: “Recipient will protect Confidential Information using the same degree of care that it uses for its own information and will not be responsible for inadvertent disclosures absent gross negligence.”  
  **Violated rule**: R-005 (Care standard must require reasonable care and be no less than care for recipient’s own *similar* confidential information; this clause removes reasonable care and disclaims liability for inadvertent disclosure absent gross negligence).

- Section 6, quote: “Confidential Information does not include information that Recipient believes from general recollection was previously known, becomes available through any source, or is independently useful to Recipient's business.”  
  **Violated rule**: R-003 (Exclusions must be narrow, documentable, and based on prior lawful knowledge, public availability without breach, independent development, or third-party receipt without duty; this clause is overbroad and subjective).

- Section 8, quote: “Upon Discloser's written request, Recipient will return or destroy Confidential Information within ninety days, except retained copies may be kept for ordinary business recordkeeping.”  
  **Violated rule**: R-008 (Return/destruction must be prompt, and retained archival/legal-hold copies must remain under continuing confidentiality obligations; 90 days is not prompt, and ordinary business recordkeeping copies lack mandated protection).

- Section 12, quote: “No right to use residual knowledge, unaided memory, or retained impressions is granted under this Agreement unless the parties approve that right in a signed writing.”  
  **Violated rule**: R-012 (Residual-knowledge clauses must be absent unless expressly approved by the client; this clause addresses residual knowledge without client approval).

No other playbook violations are present.
