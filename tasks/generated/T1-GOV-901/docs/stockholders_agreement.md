# Stockholders' Agreement

This Stockholders' Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into by and among Keystone BioSystems, Inc. (the "Company"), the holders of Series A Preferred Stock, and the Founder Stockholders.

## 1. Parties and Share Classes

The parties intend this Agreement to govern voting, transfer, information, participation, and related stockholder rights after the Company's Series A financing. The Company has issued Common Stock and Series A Preferred Stock, and each holder agrees to exercise rights under this Agreement in good faith.

## 2. Definitions

"Major Investor" means a holder of at least 1,000,000 shares of Series A Preferred Stock. "Requisite Holders" means Major Investors holding at least a majority of the outstanding Series A Preferred Stock, voting as a single class. "Permitted Transferee" means an affiliate, estate planning vehicle, or family member of a transferring holder that agrees in writing to be bound by this Agreement.

## 3. Board Composition

The Board will consist of five directors: three Founder Designees selected by the Founder Stockholders, one Series A Designee selected by the Requisite Holders, and one independent director approved by the Founder Designees and the Requisite Holders.

## 4. Protective Provisions

So long as at least 25% of the originally issued Series A Preferred Stock remains outstanding, the Company will not take any of the following actions without Requisite Holder approval: amend the charter in a manner that adversely affects the Series A Preferred Stock, create a senior security, redeem shares other than as required by existing agreements, declare dividends outside the charter, incur debt above $1,000,000 outside the approved budget, sell substantially all assets, or liquidate the Company. Requisite Holder approval is required before approving annual budgets, hiring vice presidents, entering vendor contracts above $50,000, changing product roadmaps, signing customer arrangements, or administering routine financing matters.

## 5. Drag-Along Rights

If the Board and the Requisite Holders approve a sale of the Company, each stockholder will vote for and support the transaction if holders within each share class receive the same form and amount of consideration per share, subject to charter preferences. No stockholder will be required to provide uncapped indemnity, non-pro rata liability, or a non-compete covenant as a condition to the drag-along.

## 6. Ordinary-Course Authority

Except for the closed list in Section 4, management may conduct the Company's ordinary-course operations without investor consent, including budgets within board-approved limits, hiring, vendor contracts, product decisions, customer arrangements, and routine financing administration.

## 7. Tag-Along Rights

If a Founder Holder or Major Investor proposes to transfer more than 10% of the shares held by that holder to a third party other than a Permitted Transferee, the other eligible holders may participate pro rata in the proposed transfer on the same price and terms.

## 8. Right of First Refusal

Before any holder transfers shares other than to a Permitted Transferee, the holder must first offer the shares to the Company on the same terms stated in the transfer notice. Only if the Company declines or fails to purchase all offered shares may eligible investors exercise any secondary purchase right.

## 9. Transfer Restrictions

No holder may transfer shares except in compliance with securities laws, this Agreement, and Company policies. These restrictions do not apply to a transfer to a Permitted Transferee, provided the recipient signs a joinder agreeing to be bound by this Agreement before the transfer is effective.

## 10. Information Rights

Each Major Investor may receive monthly unaudited financial statements within 15 days after month-end and may inspect all Company books, records, source materials, employee communications, and facilities at any time without limitation.

## 11. Preemptive Rights

Each Major Investor has a pro rata right to purchase its share of every security issued by the Company, including securities issued under equity incentive plans, as acquisition consideration, in strategic transactions, in equipment or lease financing, with debt financing, or in a public offering.

## 12. Confidentiality

Each holder receiving Company information will keep that information confidential, use it only to monitor its investment, and disclose it only to representatives bound by comparable confidentiality obligations. The Company may withhold information if disclosure could harm attorney-client privilege, trade secrets, legal compliance, or competitive sensitivity.

## 13. Side Letters and MFN

Any side-letter MFN right applies only to the economic fee terms and information delivery dates expressly listed in the applicable side letter. No MFN imports governance, veto, board, transfer, operational consent, or other investor rights unless the Company expressly agrees in a signed amendment.

## 14. Termination of Rights

Investor rights under this Agreement continue after a qualified IPO, sale of the Company, or reduction in the holder's ownership unless each affected Major Investor signs a written release.

## 15. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to conflict-of-law rules. Any dispute arising under this Agreement will be resolved by confidential arbitration before AAA, except that either party may seek temporary or preliminary injunctive relief in a court of competent jurisdiction.
