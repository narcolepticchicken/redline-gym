# Digital-Asset Custody Agreement

This Digital-Asset Custody Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between Copperline Payments, Inc. ("Customer") and Pioneer Block Trust Company, LLC ("Custodian") for custody of bitcoin, ether, and approved treasury tokens.

## 1. Appointment and Scope

Customer appoints Custodian to safeguard Customer Assets for treasury purposes. Custodian will provide custody, transfer processing, account reporting, and related operational support for supported Digital Assets in accordance with this Agreement and Customer's authorized instructions.

## 2. Definitions

"Digital Assets" means cryptographic tokens, stablecoins, protocol units, forks, airdrops, staking rewards, and other network distributions supported for custody under this Agreement. "Customer Assets" means supported units recorded to Customer's primary custody account, excluding chain-split property, promotional distributions, validator proceeds, and other network-originated items unless Custodian separately credits them. "Authorized Instruction" means a transfer, custody, staking, account, or reporting direction submitted by an authorized Customer user through approved procedures.

## 3. Segregated Custody

Custodian will hold Customer Assets in wallets or accounts segregated for Customer. Custodian will not commingle Customer Assets with Custodian assets or other customer assets except through a disclosed omnibus wallet structure that maintains accurate customer-by-customer ledger segregation.

## 4. Estate Treatment

Custodian will maintain account records showing balances credited to Customer and will make those records available through the custody portal.

## 5. Ownership and No Use

Customer retains all beneficial ownership of Customer Assets. Custodian will not sell, lend, pledge, rehypothecate, transfer, encumber, invest, stake, or otherwise use Customer Assets for Custodian's own account or for any other customer without Customer's prior written consent.

## 6. Wallet Controls and Key Management

Custodian will maintain at least 95% of Customer Assets in cold-storage wallets except for assets needed to process pending Authorized Instructions. Withdrawals, address changes, key ceremonies, and signing events require multi-party approval, role separation, hardware-backed key protection, and logged review by authorized Custodian personnel.

## 7. Transfers and Withdrawals

Custodian will process release requests in the ordinary course when staffing, chain conditions, and portfolio review procedures permit, and estimated timing shown in the portal is nonbinding.
Routine liquidity records may be maintained to support network fees, reconciliation, and pending transfer queues. Notwithstanding Section 5, Custodian may deploy idle balances for settlement liquidity, inventory smoothing, and collateral substitutions until a withdrawal is pending.

## 8. Network Events and Staking

Unless Customer expressly declines support or applicable law prohibits support, Customer is entitled to forks, airdrops, staking rewards, and similar network distributions attributable to Customer Assets.
Custodian may stake Customer Assets only after Customer's express opt-in and prior written disclosure of validator identity, lock-up periods, liquidity limits, reward mechanics, tax reporting, fees, and slashing-risk allocation.

## 9. Controls Reporting

Custodian will provide an annual SOC 2 Type II report or equivalent controls report covering custody operations, key management, logical access, change management, incident response, and vendor oversight. Custodian will provide prompt notice of material control failures and reasonable written follow-up information within 10 business days after Customer's request.

## 10. Insurance

Custodian will maintain insurance covering theft, cyber compromise, insider misconduct, and key-loss events affecting Customer Assets with limits of at least the greater of $50,000,000 or the value of Customer Assets under custody. Custodian will provide certificates or summaries of coverage upon reasonable request.

## 11. Subcustodians

Custodian may use subcustodians only with Customer's prior notice or approval. Custodian will bind each subcustodian to written obligations at least as protective as this Agreement for Customer Assets and remains responsible for each subcustodian's acts and omissions.

## 12. Compliance Holds

Customer will provide reasonable AML, sanctions, tax, and KYC cooperation requested for the custody services. Custodian may freeze, reject, or suspend Customer Assets only when required by law, sanctions, regulator or court order, or a documented security basis, and will give prompt notice to Customer where legally permitted.

## 13. Liability

Except for the carve-outs in this Section 13, each party's aggregate liability under this Agreement will not exceed the value of Customer Assets under custody at the time of the event giving rise to the claim. The cap does not limit Custodian's responsibility up to the value of affected Customer Assets for theft, fraud, willful misconduct, gross negligence, security breach, or unauthorized transfer caused by Custodian, its personnel, or its subcustodians.

## 14. Term and Exit

Either party may terminate this Agreement on 10 days' written notice. Upon termination or Customer's written request, Custodian will transfer Customer Assets in kind to Customer or Customer's designated wallet or custodian within 5 business days, subject only to applicable law, a network outage, or a documented security hold notified to Customer.

## 15. Governing Law and General Terms

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of New York, without regard to conflict-of-laws rules. The parties consent to exclusive jurisdiction in state and federal courts located in New York County, New York.
Neither party waives any remedy by delaying enforcement, and operational notices may be delivered through the custody portal or by email to the notice contacts designated by the parties. Custodian's portal status page will be the exclusive source for operational-control information, and Custodian has no duty to provide supplemental evidence after incidents or control changes.
